# Hopnest Glamping: How the Business Setup Works
*A Guide for the Family Farm Partnership*

To ensure the new glamping venture is successful, protects the family farm, and remains fair to all partners, we are setting it up as a separate Limited Company (**Hopnest LTD**). 

Here is a simple breakdown of how this setup works, how the money is handled, and why it is the safest and most profitable option for the farm partnership.

---

## 1. How the Setup Works (The 3 Key Elements)

### A. The Land Lease (The Rent)
* **What happens:** The farm partnership leases the glamping site (approx. 1–2 acres) to **Hopnest LTD** on a commercial lease.
* **The Rent:** 
  * **First Season (2026):** Rent-free (a "rent holiday" while we set up, launch, and establish guest bookings).
  * **From 2027 onwards:** Rent will be set at **5% of gross booking revenues** (turnover-linked rent). 
* **Why it works:** If we have a quiet year, the rent drops to protect the business. If we succeed and make £100,000, the farm gets £5,000. If we reach £200,000, the farm gets £10,000. It is completely transparent and tracked easily through the booking system.

### B. The £40,000 Startup Capital (The Loan)
* **What happens:** The farm partnership has already paid for groundworks, shepherd's huts, and equipment. 
* **How it is structured:** Instead of transferring £40,000 cash, the farm partnership **sells** these glamping assets to **Hopnest LTD** on credit. This creates a "paper loan"—Hopnest LTD legally owns the assets but owes the farm partnership £40,000.
* **Repayment:** Hopnest LTD repays the farm partnership over **5 years starting in 2027** (e.g., £8,000 per year), paid in the autumn when booking revenue is at its peak.
* **VAT Reclaim:** Because the farm partnership is VAT-registered, we will structure this sale so that Hopnest LTD registers for VAT and reclaims the 20% VAT on these startup costs directly from HMRC, ensuring no tax is lost.

### C. Daily Operations
* **What happens:** **Hopnest LTD** runs the business. It has its own business bank account, takes all the bookings, pays for the laundry, cleaning, booking fees, and maintenance. 
* **Why it works:** The farm partners do not have to do any daily work (no guest management, no hot tub cleaning) but still receive rent and loan repayments.

---

## 2. Why this is the Best Option for the Farm Partnership

### 🛡️ Reason 1: It Protects the Farm from Lawsuits (Ring-Fencing)
* **The Risk:** In a partnership, all partners share "joint and several liability." If we run the glamping site under the farm partnership and a guest suffers a severe accident (e.g., a hot tub incident or a fire in a hut) and sues, the lawsuit goes against the *entire farm*. This puts the farm’s land, buildings, machinery, and your personal homes at risk if the insurance has a loophole.
* **The LTD Shield:** By running the glamping under **Hopnest LTD**, any legal liability is strictly locked inside the company. The maximum risk is limited to the company’s assets (the huts and booking cash). The core farm land and partnership assets are **100% legally protected**.

### 🚜 Reason 2: It Protects the Farm’s Inheritance Tax Relief (APR)
* **The Risk:** Working farms qualify for **Agricultural Property Relief (APR)**, which allows farm land to pass down generations tax-free. However, if you use farm land for commercial glamping, it loses agricultural status and loses APR. 
* **The LTD Shield:** By having a clean commercial lease between the farm and Hopnest LTD, we keep the farm partnership’s status as a clean agricultural business. Mixing leisure activities directly inside the farm partnership can muddy the waters and risk a massive inheritance tax bill for the family farm later.

### 💰 Reason 3: It Generates a Massive Return on the Land
* **The Comparison:** Standard agricultural rent in the UK for grazing or crops is about **£80 to £150 per acre per year**. 
* **The Return:** By leasing the glamping area for 5% of bookings, the farm partnership will make £3,000 to £10,000 per year on just 1–2 acres. This is **30 to 50 times** the land’s normal agricultural earning power, with zero daily management effort required from the other partners.

### 📊 Reason 4: It Keeps the Farm Accounts Clean
* Glamping involves hundreds of small transactions (guest deposits, cleaning wages, booking system fees, toilet rolls). Running this inside the farm partnership makes the farm accounts messy and increases accounting fees. Having a separate company with its own bank account keeps the farm’s agricultural bookkeeping completely separate and simple.
